    THE MISSION



    Gouvernment Commission

    Our mission is to build a community around the common goal of leveraging open data to enable creative solutions to make an impact on our society. As an outcome it will further stimulate and support government agencies in their ongoing work aimed at making more data available.

    In 2018, as part of the process of increasing the digitalization of the public sector, the government put focus in open data and data-driven innovation. Public sector, academic sector, enterprizw and creative organizations from civil society need to interact and collaborate. Through open and data-driven innovation, more and more people can contribute to tomorrow's digital welfare society and together create social benefits. Increased re-use of open data contributes to increased growth, participation and efficiency. The government further argues that where there is social benefit, the public should be included as an active partner. Access to authority data, provided that these data have been made available as open data, can lead to a variety of benefits for both society and the economy. This includes giving citizens, businesses and civil society the opportunity for increased and more democratic involvement in the authorities' activities, for example in policy design and implementation, but also in transparency in government decisions. Government advocates the conscious use of open source solutions, standards and test beds.

    One of the government's objectives is a more open management that supports innovation and participation. Authorities should actively seek to enable effective re-use of public information to facilitate the emergence of an information market and to help strengthen people's self-government and exercise of human rights. If public data is published for reuse, this may be used to e.g. construct new e-services. One way to promote re-use of data is to arrange competitions in the form of so-called hackathons.
